About Me

Currently, at CERN's Radiation Protection Group, my expertise in Monte Carlo simulations is applied to assessing and mitigating radiation hazards and risks at the forefront of particle physics. My focus on the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and the exploration of physics beyond collider experiments underscores my commitment to ensuring the safety and success of groundbreaking scientific endeavors.

In the past, I was deeply involved with the International Linear Collider (ILC) experiment at DESY and the University of Hamburg. During this time, I pioneered modeling efforts for hadronic shower shapes, specifically within the CALICE-AHCAL. This model was instrumental for rapid simulation studies, including GFLASH, and also enhanced Particle Flow Studies such as Pandora PFA.

My core research revolves around the development of sophisticated detectors. This encompasses not just projects like CALICE-AHCAL, but also innovations in tomography. A recent and notable endeavor of mine has been investigating the prospective uses of muon tomography for space-based imaging, a venture I embarked upon in collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA).

Beyond these specialized projects, I harbor a keen interest in machine learning and data science. As an active member of the Young High Energy Physicists Association (yHEP), I am continuously seeking avenues to drive forward and contribute to the next generation of technological advancements.

Outside of my professional endeavors, I am an avid enthusiast of outdoor sports. I enjoy cricket, swimming, and cycling, always relishing the balance they provide to my rigorous academic pursuits.

Current Work

I currently work at the Radiation Protection Group at CERN, where I specialize in evaluating and mitigating radiological risks associated with high-energy particle physics experiments. My primary focus lies in conducting detailed Monte Carlo simulations using FLUKA to analyze radiation fields, shielding performance, and material activation for accelerator facilities including the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). These studies support safety assessments for ongoing operations and major future upgrades, helping ensure compliance with radiation protection standards and the well-being of personnel and the environment.

Previous Works

Muon Tomography for Lunar & Martian Exploration

In collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA), I explored the feasibility of using muon tomography for subsurface imaging of the Moon and Mars. Using GEANT4-based simulations, this project investigated how cosmic rays and backscattered radiation could detect underground water or ice, with potential applications in future autonomous space missions.

AHCAL Group at SPS-CERN TestBeam Campaign

Participated in beam tests at the CERN SPS as part of the CALICE collaboration, validating detector performance and collecting high-granularity calorimeter data for further analysis.

CALICE-AHCAL Technological Prototype

Contributed to the development of a highly granular sampling calorimeter for future lepton collider experiments. This prototype, based on a steel-scintillator design, used embedded SiPM-on-tile technology and integrated low-power front-end electronics for precision measurement of hadronic showers.

SiPM-on-Tile Technology

Implemented and characterized scintillator tiles with central dimples, optimized for direct SiPM coupling and enhanced light collection, supporting high-resolution calorimetric detection.

CALICE-AHCAL Event Display

Developed visualizations of hadronic shower topologies recorded in the AHCAL calorimeter, facilitating detector studies and aiding in simulation validation efforts.

3D Modeling of Hadronic Showers

Created a semi-empirical model to describe the spatial and energy development of hadronic showers in highly granular calorimeters, enhancing simulation performance for fast reconstruction algorithms.

Systematics of Alpha Decay Half-Lives

Analyzed and refined phenomenological models (Viola-Seaborg, B.A. Brown) to study correlations between Q-values and alpha decay half-lives in nuclei, optimizing theoretical parameters for better alignment with experimental data.
